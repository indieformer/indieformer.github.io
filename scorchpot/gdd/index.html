1 · Overview

One-liner

A dice roguelike about feeding a dragon for twenty years. Build an island, roll dice, harvest ingredients, cook recipes, feed the dragon. Miss a meal and the run ends.

Design pillars
  • Cozy on top, ruthless underneath. The surface is warm and hand-drawn; the systems punish sloppy play.
  • Balatro-style math, but with strategy. Multiplicative scoring, but placement and planning matter as much as the roll.
  • Broken combos, on purpose. Designed for dopamine hits and screen-shaking numbers.
  • Press your luck. The dice are the greed mechanic — winning earlier pays more, and every roll is a chance to overreach.
Genre & comps

Dice roguelike · roguelite · board-game. Nearest comps: Balatro, Slay the Spire. Single-player. PC (Steam) at launch; mobile (iOS/Android) late 2026.

Target player

Board game lovers and roguelike/lite players — people who've played Balatro or Slay the Spire. They want depth and replayability, like unlocking things, and enjoy humor and meta references. Comfortable with casual sessions, and drawn to hand-made games.

2 · Core Loop

The loop

1.BuildPlace and rearrange buildings on the island.
2.RollRoll the dice.
3.HarvestCollect ingredients.
4.CookCook with recipes — leads automatically into feeding.
5.FeedFeed the dragon. Meet the hunger target or the run ends.

The loop is not strictly sequential. After harvesting there's still an option to go back to the shop before cooking. Cooking is the one hard transition — it leads automatically into feeding.

Seasons

Each year contains multiple seasons. The player gets multiple tries at rolling and harvesting the board within the year to reach the dragon's number — a season is one of those tries. The shop does not reset on seasons. Unused seasons pay out as Prosperity (see Scoring).

Between years & during seasons

The shop is the dragon's hoard: tools, recipes, buildings, and dice, for a price. It opens between years and during seasons. Between years is also where building and rearranging the island happens — it's part of the core loop, not a separate phase.

Run structure

A completed run is 20 years, with each year getting more difficult to pass as the dragon's hunger increases. 20 years is the base — the player can continue forever after that. Depending on the difficulty, the dragon may have a tantrum that affects the board.

Meta-loop

Full meta-progression and unlock system for recipes, dice, and buildings, plus achievements. Ascension difficulty detailed in §4.

3 · Systems & Mechanics

Dice

Standard 6-sided dice. Some are colored, giving more yield. All dice can be fixed with tools that change the numbered faces. Special dice grant a permanent bonus when a certain number is rolled.

All dice are purchased through the shop, and can be sold later to replace with another. The dice tray grows with the number of harbors built on the board — starting at 3 dice and going up to 6.

The island (board)

Hex tiles are each a random food type that the player harvests from. Buildings are placed at the intersections of tiles:

  • Farmsteads yield from adjacent tiles.
  • Shrines give bonuses to neighbouring buildings.
  • Trade posts are more independent, but still work with buildings next to them.
  • Harbors get bonuses from adjacent tiles; harbor upgrades affect tools, recipes, and the shop — and each harbor grows the dice tray.

50+ buildings total.

Scoring — Fodder × Flavor × Essence × Prosperity

Fodder is what the player harvests from the board. Flavor comes from certain buildings and recipes; so does Essence. Recipes are activated to make all of these stronger. Prosperity — the number reward for not using all of your seasons — multiplies everything last, as a final bonus.

The board can be played in many ways: maximising fodder, or not collecting any harvest at all. 80+ recipes.

The dragon

Sets the hunger target each year; hunger increases every year. There are multiple dragons, each with 10 ascension levels (see §4). Snarky in tone. Can be petted.

Tantrums occur depending on difficulty and interfere with the run directly. They can: destroy buildings, lock the shop, lock a face on a die, scorch a number, scorch an ingredient making it useless — and many more hanger tantrums.

Economy — ducats, the hoard & tools

Ducats are the currency, earned primarily through buildings — though some dice and some recipes give gold too.

The shop is the dragon's hoard: sells tools, recipes, buildings, and dice. Opens between years and during seasons; stock does not reset on season change. Dice can be sold back to fund replacements.

Tools are one-off tasks: changing the face of a die, adding a number to a hex tile, or skipping forward a season for extra gold.

4 · Progression & Difficulty

In-run escalation

Each year is harder to pass as the dragon's hunger increases. On higher difficulties, tantrums interfere with the board directly (see §3, The dragon).

Difficulty design

10 ascension levels per dragon. Beating an ascension level unlocks the next one for all dragons. Run length is anywhere from 10 minutes to multiple hours depending how far the player pushes — 20 years is just the base; continuing forever is allowed.

No fixed win-rate targets: outcomes depend on how well the player understands the systems. The tutorial carries the learning load, and playtest feedback says it does that job well.

Unlocks & meta-progression

Full meta-progression and unlock system for recipes, dice, and buildings. Achievements on top.

5 · Narrative & World

Premise

You struck a deal with a dragon: twenty years of protection for twenty years of dinner service. The island thrives under the beast's watchful eye — as long as the meals keep coming.

Tone

Casual, medieval, hand-drawn, warm but with teeth. Humor lives in the dragon's dialogue and the absurdity of the recipes. Multitudes of depth available for anyone willing to dig deep.

Story delivery

Story really only exists in the tutorial. Reaching 20 years gives a "you win" with a stat screen and the option to continue or go back to menu. Beyond that, the dragon's lines are sometimes tied to being fed too little or too much, plus random quips.

6 · Art Direction

Direction

Everything hand-drawn by Daniela. Classic medieval — biblical wall-art sort of territory (illuminated manuscripts, church frescoes). Parchment-and-ember palette (this page borrows it). Reference assets held by Josh; Daniela owns the style.

8 · UX / UI

Onboarding

Fully-voiced tutorial with a custom introduction: the UI is revealed slowly to the player, explaining all the aspects needed to play. Playtest feedback on it has been good.

Input & Steam Deck

Basic Steam Deck support and controller support both in. Could do better with testing coverage before launch.
