Showcase: Nov 25

It was a tighter field than usual, tucked between haunted homes, retro collectathons, pixel-dust mining, tactical grid chaos, and rhythm-driven mayhem — but the games that made the cut show why indies stay undefeated, even in the odd months.

showcase·Issue #56 1 Dec 2025
SHOWCASE

November 2025 Highlights

This is our third Showcase since Waypoint went live — which means these picks are now highlights from a much bigger pool. Over November we added:

  • 20 Showcase games

  • ✦ 6 Hidden Gems

  • 14 Extras

Here’s just a handful of the wonderful releases you shouldn’t miss:

Windswept

Q-UP

A perfectly fair competitive game — determined entirely by a coin flip. From the creators of Universal Paperclips comes a chaotic satire of eSports, RNG, and late-stage capitalism.

$9.99 | PC, Mac

✦ Unpetrified: Echoes of Nature

Shape the world using the Golem’s abilities, uncover forgotten ruins, and watch nature bloom as your bond with a curious fox deepens.

$19.99 | PC

Windswept

A retro-spirited collectathon that looks sweet but absolutely has teeth. 40+ stages packed with secrets, tricky platforming, and those “try again… okay one more time… alright one more time” loops we all grew up on.

$19.99 | PC, Switch, PlayStation, Xbox

Foolish Mortals

A cozy, macabre mystery set on a haunted Louisiana island in the 1930s. Explore a manor stuck in time, chat with a house full of ghosts, and untangle a decades-old disappearance as you hunt for a long-lost treasure.

$19.99 | PC, Mac

✦ Deep Space Exploitation

A gritty space-mining roguelike where every asteroid is money, danger, and moral compromise wrapped in pixel dust.

$8.99 | PC

It Takes a War

A brutally “realistic” team shooter that takes itself just a little too seriously — on purpose. Strap in for 60 minutes of relentless teamwork, visceral gunplay, and the most revolutionary mechanic of all: voice chat.

$5.99 | PC

Sektori

A high-voltage twin-stick shooter where rhythm meets chaos. Blast through shifting arenas pulsing with hard-hitting techno, weaving between waves of enemies and colossal evolving bosses.

$14.99 | PC, PlayStation, Xbox

✦ Yes, My Queen

A dungeon-crawling twist on chess where every match is a puzzle, a brawl, and a little bit of chaos. Recruit pieces, outmaneuver escalating enemies, and unleash rule-breaking cards—from shifters and zombie plagues to nukes and propaganda campaigns—until the board bends to your will.

$12.99 | PC, Mac

Kingdoms of the Dump

An SNES-styled RPG born from the minds of two janitors, set in a fantastical world of garbage. Guide Dustin Binsley, the Trash Can Knight, through dank dungeons, creative kingdoms, and grid-based battles against the toxic grimelin army.

$19.99 | PC, Mac, Linux

Morsels

Scurry from sewer scraps to magical mayhem in this quirky roguelite where you collect and swap between oddball creatures mid-battle.

$14.99 | PC, Mac

These highlights only scratch the surface. Waypoint has the complete calendar of November 2025’s indie releases, extras, and even more hidden gems.

Happy gaming!
