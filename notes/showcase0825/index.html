Monthly Showcase

From wordless journeys and rhythm-fuelled roguelikes to cult classics and cozy sims, these are August’s standout indies—each one brimming with craft, heart, and a reason to hit ‘play.

showcase·Issue #42 1 Sep 2025
SHOWCASE

THIS MONTH’S TOP PICKS

Carefully curated indie releases that deserve your attention from August, 2025.

Metavoidal

Herdling

Guide a herd of gentle, horned creatures through forests, mountains, and snow-choked valleys. With no words spoken, the journey tells its story through companionship, puzzles, and the quiet rhythm of migration.

$24.99 | PC, Switch, Xbox, PlayStation

Sword of the Sea

Glide a hoversword across rolling dunes and glassy waters on a quest to restore a lost ocean. Wrapped in a meditative, atmospheric journey from the artist behind ABZÛ, The Pathless, and Journey.

$29.99 | PC, PlayStation

✦ Oscuro Blossom's Glow

2D puzzle-platformer where Selene absorbs and emits flower-light to power mechanisms and alter the environment. Hand-drawn presentation, gentle music, and decisions that can shape the world’s fate.

$5.99 | PC, PlayStation

The Dark Queen of Mortholme

A fixed-screen pixel art duel of evolving attacks, branching dialogue, and multiple endings, all designed to hit hard in a single, unforgettable sitting.

$5.99 | PC

OFF

The cult RPG finally lands on Steam: you are the Batter, tasked with “purifying” an uncanny world while the cryptic Judge watches. Expect turn‑based battles, puzzles, and a surreal trip through eerie zones that linger long after the credits.

$14.99 | PC, Switch

✦ Metavoidal

You’re a drummer trying to escape an eldritch metal band—fight through a corrupted church, collect records, and build a playlist to imbue your drumsticks with elemental powers.

$14.99 | PC, Mac, Xbox

Öoo

A text‑free, 2–3 hour exploration puzzle‑platformer starring a bomb‑toting caterpillar. Discover inventive bomb uses to clear paths, uncover secrets, and traverse a cute, quietly mysterious world.

$9.99 | PC

Is This Seat Taken?

A cozy logic puzzler about arranging fussy characters by their preferences—across cinemas, buses, weddings, and more—so everyone’s happy.

$9.99 | PC, Mac, Linux, Switch, iOS, Android

✦ Bossfight Tactics

Flip the script and fight against your own past parties in a side-scrolling, turn-based roguelike. Build wild item synergies from a pool of 100+ quirky tools—everything from bombs to passive buffs.

$5.99 | PC

Driftwood

Race as Eddy the sloth in a downhill longboarding adventure built on speed and flow. Drift sharp corners, pull tricks, and dodge cars across 15+ levels, with or without friends.

$19.99 | PC

BLACK SOULS

Awaken as a nameless undead in a mist‑choked empire twisted by fairy‑tale nightmares. Explore openly, choose a class, fight with a tense Active-Time Battle (ATB) system, and weave through multiple endings where death isn’t the end. It’s grim, strange, and obsessively detailed.

$13.99 | PC

✦ Deep Sleep: Labyrinth of the Forsaken

Point-and-click horror set inside lucid dreams. Dive into shifting dreamscapes to search for the brother you thought was dead, reshaping environments to solve puzzles while something in the maze hunts you.

$19.99 | PC

Knightica

A turn‑based roguelike autobattler where you build a warband, place and rotate units, and chase ruthless synergies. Your choices shape each run, and no two line‑ups play the same.

$14.99 | PC, Switch, Xbox, PlayStation

Tiny Bookshop

Cozy narrative-management: stock a seaside second-hand book van, pick daily locations, chat up locals, and decorate your stall.

$19.99 | PC, Mac, Linux, Switch

✦ Quartet

This 20–30 hour RPG weaves four opening tales into a single party, with fast, turn-based combat built around seamless swaps between frontline and reserve characters.

$19.99 | PC, Mac, Linux
