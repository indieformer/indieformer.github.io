THIS MONTH’S TOP PICKS

Carefully curated indie releases that deserve your attention from July, 2025.

Fretless - The Wrath of Riffson

The Wandering Village

Build a self-sustaining township on the back of a roaming colossus, juggling crops, research, and symbiosis while toxic spores nip at beast and settlers alike. Every choice steers your wandering sanctuary’s fate.

$29.99 | PC, Mac, Linux, Xbox, Switch, PS4

ISLANDERS: New Shores

Zen city-building distilled to serenely colour-popping tiles: drop farms, temples, and festivals across minimalist islands, chase high scores, then sail on when space—or ambition—runs dry.

$9.99 | PC, Mac, Xbox, PlayStation, Switch

The King is Watching

Your royal gaze is the only resource that matters—look at farms, mines, or barracks to make them work, juggling expansion and defence in a roguelite city-builder where blinking is a luxury.

$14.99 | PC

Atomic Owl

Become the last Bladewing, slicing techno-birds with a possessed katana in a neon sky-dojo. Air-dashes, parries, and shapeshifting feathers turn every screen into a hyperkinetic, feather-flecked bullet ballet. 

$19.99 | PC

✦ KILLBEAT

Guns groove to the BPM in a rhythm-shooter roguelike loaded with weapon synergies, freestyle reload solos, and sinkhole arenas that pulse beneath your boots, turning firefights into room-scale dance breaks. 

$9.99 | PC, Linux

One-Eyed Likho

Slavic folk horror done in stark greyscale. Pick your way through a freezing 19th-century Russia, lighting matches to solve burn-puzzles while the one-eyed curse from an old fairy tale stalks the tree-line.

$19.99 | PC

Raining Blood: Hellfire

CoD-Zombies chaos meets bullet-hell ferocity: scavenge mid-onslaught upgrades, kite demon hordes, die, repeat, and inch ever closer to that late-night, screen-melting, leaderboard-chasing ultimate power trip.

$7.99 | PC

✦ Hellkind

Awaken in a Galician forest where forbidden rites leak into reality; explore candle-lit ruins, solve folk puzzles, and decide whether to finish—or foil—the summoning dripping ash into your veins.

Free | PC, Mac

The Drifter

A hitchhiker’s wrong place, wrong time spirals into body-horror time loops and sweaty ’70s Ozploitation vibes; click, twin-stick, or just white-knuckle through crunching pixel set-pieces and a synth-soaked mystery. 

$19.99 | PC, Mac, Linux

and Roger

Spend an hour in a young girl’s shifting psyche, piecing half-glimpsed scenes into meaning while reality flickers like a jammed VHS; perception is narrative, doubt the only map.

$4.99 | PC, Mac, Switch

Fretless - The Wrath of Riffson

Turn-based guitar-PG where deck-built riffs trigger rhythm QTEs; jam legendary axes, stomp pedals, and shred corporate metal goons to restore harmony—hit the notes or face flat, bruising silence.

$14.99 | PC

✦ A Chamber of Stars

Trip through surreal six-track realms where rhythm duels and adventure puzzles remix a fallen rock star’s story; off-beat choices literally rewrite the album and warp the very stages you fight on.

$14.99 | PC, Mac

Gaucho and the Grassland

Tend cattle, lasso spirits, and strum camp-fire songs as a Latin cowboy drifting between pampas, beaches, and pastel dream-realms where every favour plants new roots for your homestead.

$19.99 | PC

✦ Misc. A Tiny Tale

Wholesome 3-D collect-a-thon starring pint-sized robots Buddy and Bag Boy. Scrub messes, recycle trash, and platform across milk cartons and rulers to restore joy to a backyard-scale post-human world. 

$19.99 | PC, Switch

DOGWALK

Lumber through snowy campgrounds as a golden goof, dragging your pint-sized human to find trinkets for a snowman. Be a menace or a good boy—either way, winter’s sandbox wags back.

Free | PC, Mac, Linux

Astro Prospector

Blast cofferoids for caffeine ore, pump profits into bullet-hell turrets, and flip an idle grinder into a swaggering star-miner—all to deliver humanity’s next cup of AstroCoffee.

$4.99 | PC

Hell Clock

A diabolical mash-up of Diablo loops and Hades speed set in 1896 Brazil: slaughter zealots, reroll cursed builds on the fly, and race a ticking hellfire clock through dynamic dungeons.

$19.99 | PC

Logic Bombs

160 grids of pure deduction ask you to trace chains, mark detonators, and defuse bombs with nothing but logic; complexity escalates gently, then mercilessly, until your brain sparks harder than the fuses. 

$4.99 | PC

✦ CIPHER ZERO

Toggle hexes, chase glyph logic, and let a reactive chill soundtrack bloom as 300+ minimalist puzzles teach rules by revelation; every solved board subtly reshapes the world around you.

$14.99 | PC, Mac
